Fireside Reflections

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

I think what EDA is asking is that we try to function with a "heard mentality" meaning the more we stick together and look after each other the safer we will be. When applied to this situation it means that we all need to start off willing to stick together and share our knowledge, to learn new ideas and tricks from others and in return share our own ideas and tricks.

What are your impressions

My impression so far is that if you dont come into this course already suited up in determination you not going to go far. Its clear that this course will be intense. your best shot and getting through and reaping the benefits is to make the concious decision to work hard and see it through before you go in.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I think i will find this syle of learning benificial. You never really have to feel like you will be struggling alone. I think the way the course is structured to support students is inovative and effective. If you are stuck on something and go to another student/tutor for help but their explaination doesnt really help, you can try another person and they may have another way to explain the issue which works for you, everyone sees things in a diffrent way.

Have your expectations of EDA chaned? if so, how?

Not particullarly, I had a pretty good idea what i wwas getting myself into.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I was already nervious and excited, I am perhaps a little more nervous now about the work load in boot camp.
